COVID-19 Information

Stay informed about the coronavirus pandemic

About COVID-19

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Most people experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without special treatment. However, some become seriously ill and require medical attention.

Use the menu above to learn about symptoms, precautions, current statistics, and contact information.

COVID-19 Symptoms

Common Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Emergency Warning Signs

Seek medical attention immediately if you experience:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion
  • Inability to wake or stay awake
  • Pale, gray, or blue-colored skin, lips, or nail beds

COVID-19 Precautions

How to Protect Yourself

  1. Get vaccinated - Vaccines are safe and effective at preventing severe illness
  2. Wear a mask - In public indoor spaces, especially in areas of high transmission
  3. Maintain social distancing - Stay at least 6 feet from others in public
  4. Wash hands frequently - Use soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  5. Use hand sanitizer - When soap and water aren't available
  6. Cover coughs and sneezes - Use a tissue or your elbow
  7. Clean and disinfect - Frequently touched surfaces daily
  8. Monitor your health - Watch for symptoms and get tested if needed

If You're Sick

  • Stay home except to get medical care
  • Separate yourself from other people in your home
  • Wear a mask around others
  • Cover your coughs and sneezes
  • Wash your hands often
  • Avoid sharing personal household items
  • Clean all high-touch surfaces daily
  • Monitor your symptoms and seek medical care if they worsen

Recent Trends

As of June 2023, most countries are seeing stable or declining case numbers. New variants continue to emerge but generally cause less severe illness than earlier strains.

Vaccination Progress

Global vaccination efforts continue:

  • Total doses administered: 5.5 billion
  • Fully vaccinated: 65% of global population
  • At least one dose: 72% of global population
